4. Recognizing and Addressing Problem Gambling

Gambling should be fun. If you or someone you know is experiencing difficulties with gambling, here are some signs to look for:

  • Spending more money or time on gambling than intended.
  • Chasing losses (trying to win back money you've lost).
  • Neglecting responsibilities (work, family, social life) due to gambling.
  • Lying about gambling habits.
  • Feeling irritable or anxious when unable to gamble.
  • Borrowing money to gamble or to pay gambling debts.

If you are concerned about your gambling habits, please take our self-assessment test available in your account settings or seek professional help.

5. Seeking Help and Support

If you feel you are developing a gambling problem, or if you know someone who might be, we strongly recommend seeking help from independent organizations dedicated to supporting problem gamblers. These organizations offer confidential advice and support:

  • Gambling Help Online (Australia): Provides free, confidential 24/7 online support and counselling. Visit www.gamblinghelponline.org.au or call 1800 858 858.
  • Gamblers Anonymous: A fellowship of men and women who share their experience, strength, and hope with each other that they may solve their common problem and help others to recover from a gambling problem. Visit www.gamblersanonymous.org.
  • Gambling Therapy: Offers online support to anyone adversely affected by gambling. Visit www.gamblingtherapy.org.
